Overview

Took an existing sheet metal enclosure design and redesigned it for injection molding, with the primary goals of reducing per-unit cost and simplifying assembly. All functional requirements were preserved.

The Problem

The sheet metal version required multiple bending operations, fasteners, and post-process finishing — all of which drove up cost and assembly time at higher volumes. Injection molding was identified as a better fit for the expected production quantities.

Approach

Reverse-engineered the functional requirements of the original enclosure, then rebuilt the geometry from scratch in SolidWorks with injection molding DFM guidelines applied: uniform wall thickness, draft angles on all faces, rib and boss placement, and gate location that minimizes weld lines in structural areas.

Outcome

Redesigned enclosure passed DFM review with no major issues. Estimated per-unit cost reduction of approximately 30% at volume compared to the sheet metal version. Assembly step count dropped from 6 to 2.
